SDLC Models

1. Waterfall Model

- A linear and sequential software development model.
- Each phase must be completed before the next phase begins.
- Once a phase is finished, it is difficult to go back and make changes.
- Requirements are clearly defined at the beginning of the project.
- Suitable for small projects or projects with fixed requirements.
- Testing is usually done after the development phase is completed.
- The development process flows step by step like a waterfall.

Phases in Waterfall Model
1. Requirement Analysis  
2. System Design  
3. Implementation (Development)  
4. Testing  
5. Deployment  
6. Maintenance  


2. Agile Model

- An iterative and incremental software development model.
- Development is divided into small cycles called sprints.
- Allows changes in requirements even during development.
- Focuses on continuous feedback from customers or stakeholders.
- Development and testing happen simultaneously in each iteration.
- Encourages team collaboration and frequent communication.
- Suitable for large, complex, and frequently changing projects.

Key Concepts in Agile
- Sprint Planning  
- Daily Stand-up Meetings  
- Continuous Integration and Testing  
- Frequent Product Releases  
- Customer Feedback and Improvements
